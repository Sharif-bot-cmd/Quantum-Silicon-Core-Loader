## The Long Version

### What MIT Means for You

| Right | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Use** | You can use QSLCL in any project—open source, closed source, commercial, or personal. |
| **Modify** | You can change the code, add features, fix bugs, or rewrite entire sections. |
| **Distribute** | You can share your modified version with anyone, anywhere. |
| **Sublicense** | You can license your modified version under any terms you choose (including proprietary). |
| **Private Use** | You can use it internally without sharing your changes. |

### What MIT Requires

| Obligation | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Copyright notice** | You must include the original copyright notice in your distribution. |
| **Permission notice** | You must include the permission notice (the MIT license text). |
| **No warranty** | You must state that the software is provided "as is" without warranty. |

**That's it. No other restrictions.**

## A Note on Adoption

### If You're Adopting QSLCL Into Your Tool

If you're from the palera1n team, a jailbreak project, a repair tool, or any third-party project, you're welcome to:

- **Fork the repo** and build your own version
- **Include QSLCL as a dependency** in your tool
- **Bundle it** with your distribution
- **Use it as a backend** for your own functionality

**You don't need my permission.** The MIT license already gives it to you.

Yes. The MIT license allows commercial use.

Yes. The MIT license allows sublicensing and proprietary use.

Yes. The MIT license requires you to include the original copyright notice.

## Why MIT?

I chose the MIT license because:

- It's the most permissive open-source license
- It allows maximum adoption
- It doesn't restrict commercial use
- It doesn't force you to share your changes
- It's simple and well-understood

**I want QSLCL to be used as widely as possible.** MIT is the best way to do that.
